In spring 2016, along with five graduate students, we redesign the website for Transitional Resources - a small licensed mental health centers in Seattle, WA.

Transitional Resources Website Redesign

Our client, Transitional Resources (TR), is a small licensed mental health center in King County, Seattle WA, offering a continuum of mental health treatment, housing, and vocational services to those who are most in need. We goal is to better communicate the organization’s purpose, services, and achievements to their target audience.

Implementing UCD process, we identified challenges, brainstormed our solutions, performed user testing, and iterated to optimize our solutions.

PROCESS & CHALLENGES

USER CENTERED DESIGN PROCESS

We started the project by interviewing the stakeholders to understand their business goals, branding, and targeted user groups. We carefully selected our research methods to maximize results based on time and budget constraints. The methods include heuristic evaluation, survey design, and analysis, interviews, web analytics.

USER RESEARCH & DATA ANALYSIS

RESEARCH ANALYSIS

We collected a total of 23 survey responses, 153 Google Analytics sessions, 6 interviews, and 22 card sorting sessions that provide insights into user habits for making donations online or looking for volunteer opportunities online, motivations for using the website to volunteer/donate or other purposes.

For card sorting sessions, we prepared 45 cards on current and future contents and gathered 22 feedback. We then used Participant-centric Analysis to get a high-level understanding of your card sort data and provides actionable insights. The card sorting results greatly helped us with information architecture.

Our research suggested our primary users will be donors and volunteers. We created personas, user tasks, and metrics. Debbie is our Donor persona. Her goal is to make a contribution with a positive impact on the mental illness/homeless community. Vince is our Volunteer persona. His goal is to volunteer at an organization that positively impacts the community. To inspire our users, we decided to highlight organization’s mission, shared values, and legitimacy of TR.

Design & Evaluate

IDEATION & PROTOTYPE

Based on our research, we started ideation session. We sketched and white boarded our idea to quickly generate as many solutions as we could. And then gradually increase fidelity to refine our design based on usability tests.

As we began building our prototype and doing usability testing, we further refined the sitemap so that sections under nav items were all on the same page and re-collapsed volunteering and donating sections. The final sitemap also has more technical considerations, such as length of top navigation.

DESIGN SOLUTIONS

DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Design principles are crucial guidelines to the team for building empathy for users. We studied a series of design principles before creating our own, such as Facebook Design Principles, Dieter Ram’s Ten Principles for Good Design etc. We then synthesized and created our own version.

Inspirational

Our design should encourage and motivate people to get involved due to the cause and impact of the organization.

Transparent

What the organization does and the impact of users’ contributions should be understandable.

Predictable

To help expedite users’ actions, our design should be consistent, simple, clear and clean

Accessible

Our design must be usable and meet the needs of people with different capacities

Design Solutions

Visual Design

A successful website should be both functional and aesthetically pleasing. In the final design stage, we explored different options on color schemes, typography, imageries, then established a design style guide for our client. The design decisions we made heavily rely on research activities we performed, which boil down to the four design principles we build for TR: Inspirational, Transparent, Predictable, and Accessible.

We studied a variety of non-profit websites to inspire and inform our design. We identified some common patterns as current best design practices. We also explored different typography combinations and finally chose Roboto as it is a modern, yet accessible, web friendly font.

Our client requests us to refresh the current branding based on their existing logo. Although we were not able to produce brand strategy due to time constraint, we added a secondary Aqua color to revive the brand image while preserving the existing recognized brand image.

Design Solutions

INTERACTIVE PROTOTYPE

After 3 rounds of visual design iterations, we produced an interactive prototype comprised of 7 unique page layouts implemented using InVision. Based on the hard work aforementioned, these are the results of our final Hi-Fi prototype. Starting from the hero images, in order to inspire, we want to use human elements and factual numbers to encourage and motivate people to get involved. To ensure transparency, we help users understand how small donations can make a real impact in improving someone’s life.
